# Sources & References

## Ancient Sources

### Primary Greek & Roman Texts

#### Drama
- Euripides. *Cyclops*, circa 408 BCE. (Only complete surviving satyr play)
- Sophocles. *Ichneutae* (Tracking Satyrs), 5th century BCE. (Fragmentary satyr play)
- Aeschylus. Various lost satyr plays (fragmentary references)
- Pratinas of Phlius. Various satyr plays (fragmentary, considered a master of the form)

#### Poetry & Literature
- Hesiod. *Catalogue of Women*, circa 7th-6th century BCE. (References to satyrs)
- Pindar. Various odes, 5th century BCE. (References to satyrs and nymphs)
- Ovid. *Metamorphoses*, 8 CE. (Book 11: Midas and Silenus; Book 6: Marsyas)
- Ovid. *Fasti*, 8 CE. (Roman fauns and rustic deities)
- Virgil. *Eclogues*, 42-39 BCE. (Pastoral poetry with rustic spirits)
- Pausanias. *Description of Greece*, 2nd century CE. (Various references to satyr myths and cult sites)
- Philostratus. *Imagines*, 3rd century CE. (Descriptions of paintings featuring satyrs)
- Lucian. *Dialogues of the Gods*, 2nd century CE. (Satirical dialogues including satyrs)

#### Philosophy & History
- Plato. *Symposium*, circa 385-370 BCE. (Alcibiades compares Socrates to Silenus)
- Herodotus. *Histories*, 5th century BCE. (Mentions of satyrs in various regions)

### Ancient Art
- Extensive Greek black-figure and red-figure pottery (6th-4th century BCE)
- Numerous vase paintings showing satyrs pursuing nymphs, drinking wine, playing music
- Roman mosaics and frescoes
- Pompeian wall paintings
- Hellenistic and Roman sculpture
